About

I am a graduate student in Applied Data Science at the University of Chicago with a focus on computational methods for healthcare. My research explores multimodal modeling, statistical inference, and interpretable machine learning systems for clinical decision support.

I am particularly interested in understanding how AI models reason — especially in high-stakes domains such as oncology and radiology.

Research Statement

My research aims to develop interpretable AI systems for healthcare and biomedical applications. I focus on multimodal models that combine images, text, and clinical data to provide accurate and explainable predictions. I am passionate about bridging the gap between AI research and real-world clinical deployment, ensuring that advanced models can safely assist healthcare professionals.
